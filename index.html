About

From Equations
to Impact

Dr. Saptadeep Biswas is a dedicated researcher and academician with a Ph.D. in Mathematics from NIT Agartala (2021–2025), awarded under the prestigious DST INSPIRE Fellowship.

His doctoral work — A Study on Intelligent Disaster Management: Implementing AI-based Prediction and Logistics Optimization Techniques — bridges artificial intelligence and real-world humanitarian operations.

Currently serving as Assistant Professor at GITAM School of Business, he brings deep expertise in evolutionary computation, multi-objective optimization, and supply chain resilience.

Research Focus

Three Pillars of
Scholarly Work

Publishing consistently in Q1 SCIE journals with impact factors up to 13.9, Dr. Biswas' research operates at the intersection of intelligent algorithms, disaster management, and optimization science.

01 ——

Intelligent Disaster Management

AI-driven frameworks for earthquake relief demand forecasting, flood prediction, seismic vulnerability mapping, and community resilience. Case studies in Türkiye and India. Published in IJDRR (Q1, IF 4.5).

→
02 ——

Metaheuristic Optimization

Designing and benchmarking novel hybrid evolutionary algorithms — integrating Differential Evolution with Gazelle, Coati, Crayfish, Grey Wolf optimizers for global and constrained engineering problems. Published in CMAME (Q1, IF 7.3) and AI Review (Q1, IF 13.9).

→
03 ——

Sustainable Supply Chains

Multi-objective optimization of agro-food networks, humanitarian logistics design using spherical fuzzy methodology, resource allocation in quarantine centers, and transportation problems. Published in JCP (Q1, IF 10.0) and ANOR (Q1, IF 4.5).
